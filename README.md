# 📊 Smart Discounting: When Does It Work and When Does It Hurt?

A Data Analytics Project using **SQL (Google BigQuery)** and **Tableau Public** to analyze how discounting affects business profitability. This project uses the Sample Superstore dataset to identify the optimal discount strategy and provide data-driven business recommendations.

---

# 📌 Project Overview

Discounting is one of the most widely used retail strategies to increase sales. However, excessive discounting can significantly reduce profitability.

This project investigates whether discounting always benefits a business or whether it reaches a point where it starts destroying profit. Using SQL for data cleaning and analysis and Tableau Public for visualization, the project evaluates discount performance across different months, quarters, product categories, and regions.

**Key Finding:**  
**Discounts above 20% consistently reduce profitability.**

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

### 1. Seasonal Objective
- Identify which months and quarters benefit from discounting and which experience reduced profitability despite discounts.
- Determine whether Q4's high profit is driven by discounts or seasonal customer demand.

### 2. Category Objective
- Identify which product categories benefit from discounts and which category should never be discounted.

### 3. Strategy Objective
- Determine the optimal discount percentage before profit turns negative.
- Identify which region responds most effectively to discounting.

---

# ❓ Key Business Questions

- In which month does a high discount lead to high profit (sweet spot months)?
- In which month does a high discount lead to low profit (danger zone months)?
- Which product category should never be discounted?
- Which category benefits most from discounting?
- Is Q4's high performance driven by discounts or customer demand?
- What is the optimal discount percentage before profit becomes negative?
- Which region responds best to discounting?

# 📊 Data Analysis

The project consists of five analytical sections:

### 1. Monthly Profit vs. Discount
Analyzed monthly profit trends to identify sweet spot months and danger zone months.

### 2. Category Profit vs. Discount
Compared product categories to determine which categories benefit from discounting.

### 3. Quarterly Profit vs. Discount
Evaluated quarterly performance to determine whether Q4 profits are driven by discounts or seasonal demand.

### 4. Discount Threshold Analysis
Identified the exact discount level at which profit becomes negative.

### 5. Regional Discount Effectiveness
Compared discount strategies across different regions to identify the most profitable regional approach.

---

# 📈 Key Findings

- Discounts remain effective only up to **20%**.
- Profit becomes negative beyond **20%** discount.
- November, December, and March are the strongest-performing months.
- January, April, and July are the weakest-performing months.
- Technology generates the highest profit with the lowest average discount.
- Furniture receives the highest average discount but produces the lowest profit.
- Q4's strong performance is driven by customer demand rather than higher discounts.
- The West region achieves the highest profitability while offering the lowest average discount.

---

# 💼 Business Recommendations

- Cap all discounts at **20%**.
- Reduce discounting on Furniture products.
- Focus promotions on Technology and Office Supplies.
- Review the Central region's discount strategy.
- Avoid unnecessary discounting during low-demand months.
- Use the West region's strategy as a benchmark for other regions.
